I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at the University of Gothenburg since fall 2023. My research interests include comparative politics, political economy, and coalition formation. In my dissertation, I examine how political parties cooperate during times characterized by economic difficulties. I particularly investigate coalition governments during hard times, focusing both on which governments parties form and how government stability affects democratic stabiliy. I do this using large-N quantitative methods, including both Western European and global data.

I have a M.Sc. in political science from Stockholm university, a B.A. in economics from Linköping university, and a B.A. in political science from Uppsala university. My thesis from Uppsala was awarded best B.A thesis of the year 2020/2021 by the Political Science Association in Uppsala.

Teaching

I teach on a number of courses at both the bachelor's and master's level. I hold several lectures about the EU in the courses "European Integration: Current Research and Theory" and "The EU as an International Actor". I also have extensive teaching experience in the area of democracy and democratization, following my involvement on the courses "Democracy and Democratization in a Comparative Perspective" and "Democracy in Theory and Practice".

I supervise theses at both the bachelor's and master's level, and I participate as a co-grader of theses each year. Students interested in supervision are welcome to reach out.
